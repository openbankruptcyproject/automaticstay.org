Key Automatic Stay Topics

  • Automatic Stay ViolationsWhat to do if a creditor ignores the stay. You may be entitled to damages and attorney fees.
  • What the Automatic Stay CoversForeclosure, repossession, garnishment, lawsuits, and collection calls - all stopped immediately.
  • Exceptions to the Automatic StayCriminal cases, certain tax actions, and domestic support obligations are not stopped by filing.
Quick tip: If a creditor violates the automatic stay - for example, by continuing to garnish wages after you file - you may be entitled to actual damages, punitive damages, and attorney fees under 11 U.S.C. Section 362(k). Document every violation.
